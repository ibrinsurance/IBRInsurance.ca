Registered Education Savings Plan (RESP)

Start saving for your child's post-secondary education today. The government contributes free money through grants — don't leave it on the table.

📞 647-745-6850💬 WhatsApp

Government Grants — Free Money

The Canada Education Savings Grant (CESG) adds 20% on the first $2,500 contributed annually — that's up to $500 per year, per child, in free government money. Over 18 years, this can total $7,200 in grants alone.

Key Facts

  • Lifetime contribution limit: $50,000 per child
  • CESG: 20% on first $2,500/year (max $500/year, $7,200 lifetime)
  • Canada Learning Bond for lower-income families — up to $2,000
  • Money grows tax-sheltered until withdrawal
  • Students typically withdraw in low-income years — minimal tax
  • Can be used for any post-secondary education in Canada or abroad

What If My Child Doesn't Go to School?

You can transfer RESP savings to your own RRSP (if you have room), transfer to a sibling, or withdraw contributions tax-free. Government grants must be returned.

Start Early

A child born today has 18 years of compound growth potential. Starting with $2,500/year from birth and collecting the maximum CESG could result in $90,000+ by age 18, depending on returns.
